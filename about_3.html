FAQS

  1. Are the CSR provisions of the Companies Act, 2013 applicable to all companies?

    The provisions of the CSR law of the Companies Act are applicable to all companies which have:
    • A net worth of rupees 500 crore or more or
    • A net turnover of rupees 1000 crore or more or
    • A net profit of rupees 5 crore or more, during any financial year.

  2. What is the meaning of 'Financial Year'?

    A financial year referred under subsection (1) of section 135 of the Act read with Rule 3(2) of Companies CSR Rule 2014, implies any of the three preceding years.

  3. Does 'Average Net Profit' refer to net profit before tax or net profit after tax?

    'Average Net Profit' refers to net profit before tax, as per section 198 of the Companies Act 2013.

  4. Can CSR expenditure be claimed as a business expenditure of the company?

    The amount spent by a company on CSR activities cannot be considered business expenditure, as mentioned in the Finance Act 2014.

  5. Can CSR expenditure be spent on activities beyond Schedule VII?

    According to the General Circular No. 21/2014, dated June 18, 2014 of the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, activities undertaken in pursuance of CSR policy must be relatable to Schedule VII of the Companies Act 2013. The items in the Schedule are wide ranging and broad-based. The General Circular also provides a list of activities that can be covered under CSR. Finally, it is for the Board of the Company to take a decision.

  6. Which are the activities that do not come under CSR?

    Activities that cannot be undertaken under CSR are:
    • CSR projects programmes that benefit only the employees of the company or their families.
    • Expenditure by the company in fulfilling the provisions of any other law or act.( such as Labour Laws, Land Acquisition act, Apprentice Act, etc.)
    • One-off events such as marathons, awards, charitable contribution, advertisements, sponsorship of TV programmes, etc.
    • Contribution to any political part either directly or indirectly.
    • Activities which are in pursuance of the company's normal course of work.
    • Activities undertaken outside India.

  7. What are the tax benefits that can be availed under CSR?

    There are no specific tax benefits that have been extended to CSR expenditure per se. This is because the Finance Act, 2014 clearly specifies that CSR expenditure cannot be considered business expenditure. Even though there is no tax exemption given to expenditure incurred on CSR, spending on activities such as contribution to PMRF, scientific research, rural development, skill development, agricultural extension, etc which are valid CSR activities, have their own exemptions under different sections of the Income Tax Act, 1961.

  8. If a company fulfils the criteria under section 135, but a subsidiary of the company does not, should the subsidiary still comply with section 135?

    Unless the subsidiary itself fulfils the criteria, it does not need to comply with section 135.

  9. Are the provisions of the CSR law applicable to a Section 8 Company, if it fulfils the criteria under section 135(1) of the Act?

    There is no exemption given to Section 8 Companies and they have to comply with the CSR law.

  10. Can the contribution of a company towards a trust, society or Section 8 Company be considered under CSR spending?

    The general Circular No. 21/2014 of the MCA date June 18, 2014 clarifies that contribution to Corpus of a trust, society or Section 8 Company will qualify as CSR expenditure as long as:
    • The trust/society/Section 8 Company is created exclusively for undertaking CSR activities or,
    • Where the corpus is created exclusively for a purpose directly relatable to activities listed in Schedule VII of the Act.

  11. Is contribution to disaster relief a valid CSR activity?

    Please refer to point no. 7 of the annexure to the General Circular date June 18 2014, issued by the MCA.

  12. Can contribution in kind be monetized to be shown as CSR expenditure?

    According to Section 135, the company has to spend the required amount in the form of money.

  13. Is it mandatory to display a company's CSR policy on its website?

    As per Section 135(4) the CSR policy f the company must be displayed on its website, after taking into account the recommendations of the CSR Committee.

  14. Is reporting of CSR mandatory in Board's report?

    The Board's report of a company qualifying under section 135(1) pertaining to a financial year commencing on or after the 1st day of April, 2014, shall include an annual report on CSR containing particulars specified in the Annexure.

  15. Is it mandatory for foreign companies to give a report on CSR activities?

    In the case of a foreign company, the balance sheet filed under sub-clause (b) of sub-section (1) of section 381 shall contain an annexure regarding report on CSR.

  16. If a company spends in excess of 2% of its average net profit of three preceding years on CSR in a particular year, can the excess amount be carried forward to the next year and be offset against the required 2% CSR expenditure of the next year?

    Any excess amount spent cannot be carried forward to the subsequent years and adjusted against that year's CSR expenditure.

  17. Can the unspent amount from out of the minimum required CSR expenditure be carried forward to the next year?

    The board is free to decide whether any unspent amount from out of the minimum required CSR expenditure is to be carried forward to the next year. However, the carried forward amount should be over and above the next year's CSR allocation equivalent to at least 2% of the average net profit of the company of the immediately preceding three years.

  18. Can involvement of employees of a company in CSR project of a company be monetized and accounted for under the head of CSR expenditure?

    Monetization of per bono services of employees will not be counted towards CSR expenditure. However, contribution and involvement of employees in CSR activities of the company will undoubtedly generate interest in the work and promote transformation from Corporate Social Responsibility as an obligation to Socially Responsible Corporate in all aspects.

  19. Can CSR funds be utilised to fund Government Schemes?

    The objective of CSR is to encourage corporate to discharge their social responsibility with innovative ideas and management skills, which yield better outcomes. Hence, it is preferred that CSR funds not be used to supplement the resource gaps in Government Schemes. Use of corporate skills in the delivery of public goods is at the core of CSR implementation by companies. In principle, CSR projects should have a larger reach than those of the Government. However, under CSR provision of the Act, the Board of the eligible company is free to take a decision on supplementing any Government Scheme, provided the scheme permits corporate participation and all provisions of Section 135 of the Act are compiled by the company.

  20. How can companies with small CSR funds take up CSR activities?

    This problem can be overcome by a proper management system and efficient selection of a relevant project. Further resources can be pooled with other companies and a joint CSR programme can be undertaken.

  21. Who is the appropriate authority for approving and implementing CSR programmes or projects of a company?

    Section 135 of the Act, Schedule VII and Companies and CSR Policy Rules, 2014, read with General Circular (18.06.2014) issued by the MCA provide the broad boundary within which eligible companies are required to formulate their CSR policies. All projects or programmes should be approved by the Boards on the recommendations of their respective CSR Committees.

  22. What is the role of the Government in monitoring implementation of CSR?

    The government has no role to play in monitoring the implementation of CSR by companies. The objective of the law is not to enforce a strict guideline but to generate a conducive environment so that companies can take up the responsibility and contribute towards human development. The existing legal provisions like mandatory disclosures, accountability of the CSR committee and the Board, provisions for the audit of the accounts of the company, etc, provide enough safeguards.

  23. Is there a mechanism to allow third parties to monitor the quality and efficacy of the CSR expenditure as well as to assess the performance of the companies?

    The Government cannot employ any third party to monitor the quality of the CSR expenditure. The respective CSR committees and the company boards themselves monitor and assess the condition.

Source: Ministry of Corporate Affairs, Government of India.
